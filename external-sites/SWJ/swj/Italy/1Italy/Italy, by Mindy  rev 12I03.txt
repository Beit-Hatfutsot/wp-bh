<div class=Section1 dir=RTL>

<h1 dir=LTR>A Jewish History</h1>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-size:28.0pt;font-family:"Monotype Corsiva";color:#993366'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Italy! <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>What comes to mind? For some it is
Chianti wine, magnificent Renaissance cathedrals, and golden Tuscan landscapes;
to others, it is Baroque</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>architecture, statues,
exquisite paintings and frescoes and rich history. These are only part of the
story. Going back to the Italian peninsula of two thousand years ago permits a
focus on the Jewish people there and how their</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>past intertwined with
that of Italy</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The Italian peninsula is the one
European region where Jews have resided without interruption since the 2nd
century B.C.E. Julius Caesar</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>granted them various
exemptions to allow them religious autonomy. Many of the 6500 Jewish prisoners
that had been sent to Rome</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>from Judea as slaves,
eventually attained their freedom and settled in Rome. Of the thirteen
synagogues known to exist in ancient Rome, the ruins</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>of one of them—from
the 1st and 4th centuries CE—has been excavated in the city's ancient port at
Ostia</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span>.</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Catacomb inscriptions and frescoes in
Rome and its outskirts suggest that some of the pagan Roman population took an
interest in</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'>Judaism. But that changed as Hellenistic elements
crept into early Christian beliefs, practices and iconography. Toleration for
the Jews ended in the late 4th century C.E., with the adoption of Christianity</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>as the
empire’s official religion. The Roman 'arena' disintegrated during more than
1,400 years, as control of the peninsula’s regions shifted among foreign
powers, local nobles and the papacy. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>During the late Middle Ages, 13<sup>th</sup>
and 14<sup>th</sup> centuries C.E., the Jewish</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>population,
concentrated in the southern regions, suffered from a range of decrees. A few
protected them while others bound them to</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>intolerable
circumstances. Forced conversions caused half of the nearly 15,000 Jews in the
south to abandon their religion. Entire</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>communities were
annihilated and many synagogues were converted to churches. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>By the turn of the 14<sup>th</sup>
century, Jews were</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'>allowed to engage in banking, since the Church
forbade money lending by Christians. Three hundred small Jewish communities
sprouted, up and down</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>the peninsula. As a
result, the two centuries of the Renaissance were marked by many Jewish
contributions to intellectual, artistic and scientific</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>endeavors, amidst a
fury of cultural activity. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>But this did not last long. Jews were</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>expelled
from several cities amid anti-Jewish propaganda. Spain, then reigning in
Sardinia and Sicily, included them in the expulsion edicts of March 1492, and
the Jews relocated to central and Northern cities of the peninsula—welcome
there for their banking experience. New <i>kehillot</i></span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>grew and matured, but
not for long. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The Counter-Reformation created an
atmosphere that encouraged the pope's harshness against the Jews,</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>confining
them to ghettos, restricting their economic activity and burning books of the
Talmud. By the 16th to the mid 17th century, the ghetto</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>was an established
institution for nearly 30,000 Jews, 7000 of them crammed into the ghetto in
Rome. Cosmopolitan intellectual life was</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>limited, but the study
of Talmud and mystic Kabbalah flourished. Napoleon’s conquest liberated the
ghetto (1796-1798) but the 1815 French</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>retreat allowed
restoration of the old repressive order</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>With the goal of ridding the peninsula
of oppressive regimes, the Risorgimento liberation movement (1750-1870)
proclaimed freedom and equality for Jews. Pope Pius IX ordered the abolition of
ghettos in Rome and other Papal States during the</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> 1848 </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>revolution.
Italy and Italian Jewry were liberated with the annexation of Rome to a united
Italy in October 1870</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span> New careers now accessible in the cities emptied many rural
Jewish communities during the decades before World War I as the Jews eagerly
integrated into the larger society. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>As Mussolini's Fascist Government</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>became
closer to Nazi Germany in 1936, so Italian Fascism turned to overt
anti-Semitism. Italy's surrender in September 1943 left the south in</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>the
hands of the Allies, but the Nazis controlled central and Northern Italy, where
they viciously applied the &quot;Final Solution.&quot; Fewer</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>then
30,000 Jews in post-war Italy found themselves struggling to rebuild
communities diminished by deportation, conversion and emigration.</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Local Italian populations had little
interest in destroying the synagogues and the Germans had had little time to
wreak the kind of damage they</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>did in other parts of
Europe. Therefore, many Italian synagogues remained intact. Most of Italy's
standing synagogues are in a reasonable state</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>of repair, including
some that are in towns without Jews. A few vacant synagogues have recently been
restored with funding by both municipal</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>and Jewish interests.
The majority of extant synagogues as in the older synagogues</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>in
Italy are in the Sephardic pattern, with bipolar arrangement of facing benches,
or</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;
mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'>with a central <i>bimah</i>. Some follow the
Ashkenazic layout, placing Ark and <i>bimah</i> together, at the east. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Today, Italy's 35,000 Jews are
concentrated in Rome and Milan, with smaller numbers in Turin, Florence and
Livorno. Some 23 other cities and</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>towns are home to the
remainder</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span>.</span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal dir=LTR style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:
embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>
