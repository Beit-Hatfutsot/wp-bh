Synagogue interior to the Heikhal. The splendor of the interior contrasts sharply with the austere façade. The entire hall and its ceiling vaults are decorated in lavish Rococo style with a multitude of inscriptions in ornate cartouches. In an unusual orientation, the spacious Heikhal and bimah occupy the south wall because of major changes introduced in the 1866 renovations. In the style of contemporary church design, the architect introduced a pulpit, accessed initially by a spiral stair, but now, only by a portable ladder.
