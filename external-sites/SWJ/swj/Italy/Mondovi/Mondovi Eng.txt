
<div class=Section1>

<p class=MsoTitle>Ghetto without a Gate</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Mondovi is a
small town located partly at the summit and partly at the foot of the </span><st1:place><st1:PlaceName><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Regale</span></st1:PlaceName><span
 style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> </span><st1:PlaceType><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Mountain</span></st1:PlaceType></st1:place><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> in the
Piedmont hills of northwestern </span><st1:country-region><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Italy</span></st1:place></st1:country-region><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>. Jews first
settled in Mondovi in 1580, after expulsions from </span><st1:country-region><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Spain</span></st1:place></st1:country-region><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> and southern </span><st1:country-region><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>France</span></st1:place></st1:country-region><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Jewish
moneylenders were vital to the local economy, charging far less interest than
the clerical loan banks. Their useful business positions and Mondovi’s distance
from </span><st1:City><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
  mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Rome</span></st1:place></st1:City><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> favored
cordial relations with the Christian community. A 1555 papal decree confined
Jews to ghettos, but the ruling Italian Savoy dukes avoided complying. Finally,
in 1720, an “open” ghetto was established in the upper town on via <span
class=SpellE>Vico</span> behind the church. As this was a major thoroughfare,
no gate was built to close it off. Continuing this lax interpretation of papal
decrees, Christians lived near the Jews and, oddly, Jews lived in proximity to
the church. After the Edict of Emancipation on </span><st1:date Year="1848"
Day="2" Month="4"><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:
 EN-US'>April 2<span class=GramE>,1848</span></span></st1:date><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>, Fortuna
Estella Levi organized Jewish and Catholic women jointly to donate clothing and
goods to families of </span><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
 mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Piedmont</span></st1:place><span style='font-family:
"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>’s soldiers fighting for Italian
independence. The harmonious relationship obtained as long as Jews lived in
Mondovi. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>The community
was always tiny. A census by Carlo <span class=SpellE>Emanuele</span> III in
1761 listed 64 individuals in 12 Mondovi Jewish families. In 1839, 79 males and
68 females were counted, although this included <i>kehillah</i> participants
from valley villages below<i>. </i>In 1869 the population had risen to 200. The
<i>kehillah</i> of </span><st1:City><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
  mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Turin</span></st1:place></st1:City><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> annexed that
of Mondovi and others in 1931. Family names associated with the town include:
Levi, <span class=SpellE>Momigliano</span>, Lattes, <span class=SpellE>Ottolenghi</span>,
</span><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:
 EN-US'>Iona</span></st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>, <span class=SpellE>Foa</span>, <span
class=SpellE>Segre</span> and <span class=SpellE>Ortona</span>. Town documents
revealed women’s personal names such as: <span class=SpellE>Giuditta</span>, <span
class=SpellE>Graziosa</span>, <span class=SpellE>Clelia</span>, <span
class=SpellE>Diamanta</span>, Sara, </span><st1:City><st1:place><span
  class=SpellE><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:
  EN-US'>Vittoria</span></span></st1:place></st1:City><span style='font-family:
"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>, <span class=SpellE>Rachele</span>,
Ester and Bona. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>During World War
II, local residents and clergy hid Jews who fled from </span><st1:City><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Turin</span></st1:place></st1:City><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> and six from
Mondovi. One of these, Dr. Marco Levi (1910 - 2000) was the last Jew who lived
in Mondovi. His forebear,<b> </b><span class=SpellE>Donato</span> Levi had
founded the Mondovi Bank of Exchange and Discount in 1820 and the bank remained
in family hands down to Marco, until a merger in 1972. Other Jewish businesses
included: raising silkworms, manufacturing and selling silk; a ceramics
industry; and an iron foundry established by engineer Giorgio <span
class=SpellE>Bassani</span> that supplied the Italian Air Force in WW I. <span
class=SpellE>Felice</span> <span class=SpellE>Momigliano</span> (1886 - 1924),
an author and politician, bequeathed his extensive book collection to Mondovi’s
municipal library.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<h1 style='text-align:justify'><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>The
Synagogue<o:p></o:p></span></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><u><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p><span
 style='text-decoration:none'>&nbsp;</span></o:p></span></u></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>The synagogue,
dating from the early 18<sup>th</sup> century, is on the third floor of an
apartment building at 65 via <span class=SpellE>Vico</span>. <span class=GramE>It’s</span>
decorative style is unique to Piedmont synagogues and survives today only in <span
class=SpellE>Chieri</span>, <span class=SpellE>Carmangnola</span> and <span
class=SpellE>Cherasco</span>, as well as Mondovi. In the center of the main
room an octagonal <i>tevah </i>(reader’s desk)<i> </i>is raised two steps above
floor level. Six wood panels around the <i>tevah</i> are carved and decorated,
leaving one face on either side open for entry. Eight pillars in trompe l’oeil<b>
</b>marble paint support an airy wooden baldachin. The same technique created
14 purple-draped “windows” on three walls and two towering Corinthian pillars
on the </span><st1:State><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
  mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Ark</span></st1:place></st1:State><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> wall.
Biblical verses inscribed on panels above the windows are clues to the
philosophy of Mondovi’s Jews. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>The <i>Heikhal</i> is on the east wall
opposite the entrance. Wrought iron railings flank the low platform approaching
the exceptional walk-in </span><st1:State><st1:place><span style='font-family:
  "Times New Roman"'>Ark.</span></st1:place></st1:State><span style='font-family:
"Times New Roman"'> A large bronze <i>hanukkia</i> stands next to the </span><st1:State><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>Ark</span></st1:place></st1:State><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'> on the platform. The </span><st1:State><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>Ark</span></st1:place></st1:State><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'> doors are carved and gilded wood,
centered between two helical columns painted with a climbing grapevine. A
seven-branched <i>menorah </i>is carved<i> </i>on the upper part of the doors
and its candles bear flames of red paint. The walk-in </span><st1:State><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>Ark</span></st1:place></st1:State><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'> juts into a rear porch that served as
the <i>heder</i>.<i> </i>A direct hit by Allied bombs on the </span><st1:City><st1:place><span
  style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'>Turin</span></st1:place></st1:City><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman"'> synagogue in World War II destroyed the
13 Torah scrolls sent from Mondovi for safekeeping.</span><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> One surviving
scroll and other artifacts were safely hidden in Dr. Marco Levi’s home one day
before the Germans arrived. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>A wooden
charity box stands on a side table in the main sanctuary, sectioned for the
poor, the school and <span class=SpellE>Eretz</span> <span class=SpellE>Yisrael</span>.
In a corner of the entrance landing, a stone funnel collects rainwater to a
stone basin for ritual hand washing. A short corridor leads to a <i>matroneo </i>(women’s
section) containing three rows of wooden benches. The women viewed the sanctuary
proceedings through open windows rather than latticed shutters. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify;text-indent:36.0pt'><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>The last
rabbi, <span class=SpellE>Ferrucio</span> <span class=SpellE>Servio</span>,
left Mondovi in 1905 when fewer than 10 families remained. Never electrified,
the synagogue was opened briefly for a wedding in 1924. In 1980, Jewish youths
from </span><st1:City><st1:place><span style='font-family:"Times New Roman";
  mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Turin</span></st1:place></st1:City><span
style='font-family:"Times New Roman";mso-fareast-language:EN-US'> assisted
Israeli <span class=GramE>architect<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'> 
</span>David</span> <span class=SpellE>Cassuto</span> in cleaning and
refurbishing the synagogue. A <i>ketubbah</i> (marriage contract) from 1750<span
class=GramE>,<span style='mso-spacerun:yes'>  </span>pages</span> of
micrography and embossed Torah crowns in 17<sup>th</sup> century Baroque style
were found in the benches of the sanctuary. <o:p></o:p></span></p>
