Lower right, repousse silver door of the Heikhal, Scuola Italiana. The central motif features a bold flower burst, highlighting a giant sunflower in a deeply fluted urn. The urn and the kidney-shaped container below it are sculpted in high relief in the grand manner of the Italian Rococo. Conical scrolls frame the inscribed medallion above the sunflower. Decorations of both lower doors are identical but the inscriptions differ. This one asserts:

In front of this door,

All men who wish

To worship the Lord

Shall raise their voices.

A precious treasure lies within

—Concealed and well hidden—

Beyond comparison with

All honor and glory.
