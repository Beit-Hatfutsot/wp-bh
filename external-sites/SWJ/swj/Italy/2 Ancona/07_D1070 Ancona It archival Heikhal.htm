The Heikhal of the Italian rite synagogue, Scuola Italiana  before it was moved in 1932. Constructed in Baroque style in 1635, the synagogue underwent Rococo renovation about a century later. This archival photograph shows the three-bay Rococo screen on pedestals and its low metal balustrade that separated the raised Heikhal from the prayer room. (Photo courtesy of the Umberto Nahon Museum of Italian Jewish Art).
