<p class=MsoTitle align=left style='text-align:left'><b><span style='font-size:
28.0pt;font-family:"Monotype Corsiva";color:#993366'>A Spark of the Divine<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span
style='font-size:28.0pt;font-family:"Monotype Corsiva";color:#993366'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Dating
from the 4th century B.C.E., Ancona was an independent city, willing, in the
Middle Ages, to augment its Jewish population in order to</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>benefit from its
skills. The port's brisk maritime commerce attracted merchants and Jewish traders
with Levantine ties. By the 14th century,</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Ancona&#8217;s Jewish
population was second in size in Italy only to that of Rome. Fifteenth century
popes levied heavy taxes on the Jews and then the</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Friars of the
Franciscan Order seriously encroached on Jewish livelihood when they
competitively expanded their money-lending network to</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>include Ancona in 1490</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;
mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The
port city was a welcome sanctuary in the 16th century for Jews who had been
persecuted in Iberia and expelled or forced to convert. When it</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>came
under the Papal State in 1532, the city eagerly invited Levantines and Jews to
help develop its commerce. Papal authorities at first</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>concurred with the
wisdom of this policy and guaranteed protection in Ancona to a hundred families
of Portuguese <i>conversos</i> (Jews forced to</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>convert, also called
&#8220;New Christians&#8221;).<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The
attitude changed drastically when the former head of the Inquisition took over
the Papacy as Paul IV (1555-1559) and launched one of the</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>most wretched periods
in the history of Italy's Jews. Decreeing a ghetto in Ancona, he forbade Jewish
worship in all but one synagogue. The</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>empty buildings were
purposely left standing, for the taxes. Conversos were the special target of
the pope&#8217;s vicious pursuit, particularly those of</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Ancona. In 1556 his
emissaries threw a hundred of them into prison and the Inquisition tribunal
condemned 50 of them to die</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The
outstanding Jewess of her day, Dona Gracia Nasi, came to their aid by
recruiting the help of Sultan Suleiman. She managed to save the</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Turkish
subjects among them, but 26 Jews were burned at the stake. This atrocity
aroused Jews throughout the Mediterranean basin to their first militant act
against the popes&#8217; excessive power. Dona Gracia initiated an effective boycott
of Ancona's port. Two years later, commercially</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>deprived and in fear
of more papal reprisals, the Jews of Ancona reluctantly terminated the boycott.
The Church adjusted its policy to extract</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>revenue at every turn.
After expelling the Jews from papal cities in 1569, it exempted from taxes a
few large <i>kehillot</i>, including Ancona. Jews still</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>had to pay taxes on
the 115 synagogues left behind in the emptied ghettos.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Because
wealth led to ostentation by Jewish merchants, importers and textile magnates,
Ancona rabbis felt it necessary in 1766 to post Hebrew</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>notices in the ghetto
to announce rules of constraint, particularly at weddings. The rabbis limited
spending on flowers, the number of dishes</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>served and the number
of guests invited&#8212;except for the invited poor. In contrast, the city's
proletarian Jews led a harsh and dreary life. They</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>were mostly peddlers,
tailors or second hand dealers</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>In
February 1797, Napoleon&#8217;s forces took Ancona. The Jews rejoiced as the French
forces broke down the ghetto walls. But again, the brunt of the victors&#8217;
taxation fell upon the Jewish community. During the French occupations of 1797
to 1799 and 1800 to 1814, the Jews enjoyed full civil</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>equality in Ancona. In
1814, with Napoleon's final retreat, the ghetto was reinstated. Hospitals
expelled wounded or sick Jews; all the former</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>restrictive conditions
were restored</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span
dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The
period of liberation and political unification of Italy, the Risorgimento,
began about 1750 and lasted until 1870. The Jewish communities</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>threw
themselves into this movement with all their soul. The Ancona ghetto was
abolished in 1830 and the 1848 uprising brought about a more</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:
Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>liberal
attitude towards the Jews. A year after the papal commander destroyed the
Levantine synagogue during a siege in 1860, the</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Risorgimento armies
liberated the region of Marche, which joined the kingdom of Italy and the Jews
of Ancona were granted full civil rights.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-bottom:12.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:
36.0pt;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Emancipated,
the Jews integrated into Italian society, with increasing frequency of
intermarriage as the years went by. Nevertheless, Ancona</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Jews took an interest
in Jewish revival and Zionism. During World War II, 400 Jews of the Ancona <i>kehillah
</i>survived by paying large sums in</span><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>ransom.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
