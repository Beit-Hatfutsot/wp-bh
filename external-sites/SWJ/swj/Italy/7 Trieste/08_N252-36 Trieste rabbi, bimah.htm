Rabbi Elia Richetti, rabbi of Trieste from 1980 to 1993, at the bimah. A giant cast menorah and a pair of oversized bronze candelabra on marble pedestals tower above the bimah. “When services close with Neilah, on Yom Kippur,” Rabbi Richetti explained, “we have about 500 people. This includes Jews from other nearby towns . . .” he paused, “and the non-Jewish spouses and family members. In the kehillah there is one old man who is the last Jew in his family. He comes alone to pray every Shabbat, but on Yom Kippur his whole non-Jewish family comes to join him.”
