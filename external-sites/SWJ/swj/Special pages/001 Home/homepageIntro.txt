<h1><span style='font-size:26.0pt'>Synagogue Art in Jewish Life<o:p></o:p></span></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span
style='font-size:28.0pt;font-family:"Monotype Corsiva"'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:Verdana'>For
centuries, </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Jews populated the
villages, towns and cities of continental Europe until the Holocaust destroyed
their communities and their lives. They no longer live in the thousands of villages,
towns and cities of their former prominent presence, but the houses, shops,
factories and community institutions that the Jewish culture had created remain
there, ownerless and unguarded</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>.</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span dir=RTL></span><span dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:
Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span><span
style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span></span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Surviving synagogue buildings
and cemeteries are often the only structures that are of recognizably Jewish
origin. The synagogues were the places most fully charged with specific Jewish
identity. Their art and architecture constitute a record of what was
esthetically important to the communities that built and used them. Synagogue
art must therefore be documented, remembered and preserved as a major visual
element of Jewish history.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>With this mission in
mind, Rivka and Ben-Zion Dorfman documented synagogue art and architecture,
particularly in the lesser-known hinterland settlements. In Austria, the Czech
Republic, Greece, Hungary</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL
style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>,</span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=LTR></span> Italy, Slovakia and the former Yugoslavia, they sought out
synagogue buildings in villages, towns and cities to record the grandeur&#8212;and
the desolation&#8212;that remained of the former Jewish presence. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>Local populations had
converted many buildings into dwellings, storerooms, churches, commercial
premises or other mundane uses. In the past two decades, however, a trend has
developed for restoration to cultural use and some are being turned into
galleries, museums, libraries and concert halls. As this site develops and
expands, it will present new and current information</span><span dir=RTL></span><span
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span> </span><span style='font-family:Verdana'>on this aspect.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:42.0pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana'>The primary source for
the present content of this Synagogues Without Jews website is the Dorfmans</span><span
dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;
mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span dir=RTL></span>&#8217;</span><span dir=LTR></span><span
style='font-family:Verdana'><span dir=LTR></span> book <i>Synagogues Without
Jews&#8212;and the communities that built and used them.</i> A growing cadre of
enthusiastic volunteers is busy researching the histories of more communities
and preparing them for posting on this site, together with photographs from the
Dorfman Archive of Synagogue Art</span><span dir=RTL></span><span lang=HE
dir=RTL style='mso-ascii-font-family:Verdana;mso-hansi-font-family:Verdana'><span
dir=RTL></span>. </span><span dir=LTR></span><span style='font-family:Verdana'><span
