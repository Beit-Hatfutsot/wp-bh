A kehillah of only some 500 Jews in Liberec, Bohemia, built the grand synagogue shown in this postcard from the collection of Bill Gross, of Ramat Aviv. The synagogue, built in 1889, dominated one of the main squares of the city and was torched by the Nazis on Kristallnacht 1938. On the same site, 52 years later, the municipality built Reconciliation House, a modern complex that includes an immense library and a small, triangular synagogue for the surviving Jewish community.
