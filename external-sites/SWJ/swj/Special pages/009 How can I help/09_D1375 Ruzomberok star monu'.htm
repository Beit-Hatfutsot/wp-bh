The neo-Moresque synagogue in Ruzomberok, in the Lower Tatras of Slovakia, was used post-war for storing print rollers of a textile factory. Scheduled for restoration but not yet funded, the building stands empty and expectant. The architectural firm of Azum (Atrium), of the Gromnica brothers, Rastislav and Martin, provided copies of their ambitious and imaginative plans for the reconstruction to a concert hall, which include a large, three-dimensional Star-of-David for the entrance plaza.
