How can I help?

This website is an all-volunteer affair and there are many ways you can help.

Comment

Let us know what you think of the site.

Tell us of your experience at any of the places we show, or places you know from other sources.

Translate

If you know any of the following languages well enough: Croatian, Czech, French, German, Greek, Hebrew, Hungarian, Italian, Russian, Slovak, Spanish, you can help us translate the content of the Synagogues Without Jews Website to that language, so that others can read it at their convenience. Contact our language editor and follow suggestions for your contribution. We’ll be back to you, with our appreciation.

Photos of synagogues or other aspects of the Jewish built heritage

If you can spare copies of your photos, drawings, etc., of synagogues or other elements of the Jewish built heritage---from any country---and would like them to be part of the Dorfman Archive of Synagogue Art and Architecture, supply the following information:

    name and contact information of photographer

    place and date of photo [and, optionally, your story].

Write “Publication permitted,” sign and send them, protected from bending, to Synagogue Art Research, POB 49022, Jerusalem 91490, Israel.

Community history

Write a page on the history of a Jewish community, provide photos of its synagogue art and architecture, or other aspect of Jewish interest and send them in for inclusion on this site.

Reciprocal links

Do you have a site relevant to our topic? How about reciprocal links?

Sponsor special projects

The book, Synagogues Without Jews, is undergoing professional translation to Hebrew. It will need a modest subsidy for publication.

The Dorfman Archive of Synagogue Art and Architecture, now in excess of 26,000 photos, must be digitized for permanence. This project is urgent, for preservation of the images.

If you wish to contribute toward these goals, please contact us for details.
