Veliki Sefardski Hram (the Great Sephardi Synagogue), 1930, the seventh Sephardi synagogue built in Sarajevo before World War II was one of the largest in the Balkans. Designed in neo-Byzantine style with neo-Moresque elements, it is crowned with an elliptical dome, 22 m high by 30 m diameter. Damaged in World War II (inset), and after many years of neglect, it was deeded to the city for cultural use. It is now the headquarters of SDA, a Moslem political party.
