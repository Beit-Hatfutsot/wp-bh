Salonika, Greece, a city that in 1904 bustled with 75,000 Jews who frequented 30 synagogues, has a depleted but active kehillah of about 1,000. The large Monasterion Synagogue, built in 1927 and one of the few that remain, serves most of the community. Among its Judaica is this Torah shield of repousse silver from the mid-19th century, with delicate floral ornamentation.
