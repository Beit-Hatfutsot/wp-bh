This imaginative, computer-generated view of the façade shows part of the plan by the local architectural firm, Atrium, of the brothers Rastislav and Martin Gromnica, for restoration of the synagogue in Ruzomberok, Slovakia. Built originally in neo-Moresque style in 1880, the building served until the late 1990s as a storeroom for a local textile factory.

 

 

In the charmingly decorated office of their architectural firm, Atrium, the brothers Rastislav and Martin Gromnica pose with their father. They may have long to wait for the funds that will enable restoration of the synagogue in Ruzomberok according to their plans.
