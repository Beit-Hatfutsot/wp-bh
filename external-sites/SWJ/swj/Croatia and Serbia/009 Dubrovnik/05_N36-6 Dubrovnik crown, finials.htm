A Torah crown and a pair of rimmonim (finials) at the bimah corner. The iron frame is there to hold the Torah scroll and ornaments during the service. The crown of repousse silver, has floral inserts in large ovals and a border of tiny florets. The silver finials are four sided, topped by a pyramid, crowned with a tiny flower or bird, and rimmed with miniature bells on chains. They suggest the shape of a medieval tower, after a Venetian prototype that became popular in Italy, north Africa, and parts of the Ottoman empire in the 18th C. These items were among the ceremonial silver loaned in 1992 to Yeshiva University Museum in New York.
