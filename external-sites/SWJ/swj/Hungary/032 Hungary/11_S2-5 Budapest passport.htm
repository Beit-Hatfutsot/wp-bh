One of the many historic items in the archive of the Rabbinical Seminary in Budapest is this “Wallenberg passport.” It was issued in July 1944 by the Swedish diplomat in Budapest to protect a Jewish woman when mass deportation was ordered for Hungarian Jewry and nearly half a million Jews of Hungary had already been deported. Wallenberg’s chief operation during those chaotic months of fall and winter was the distribution of thousands of such Swedish “Schutz-paesse” certificates of protection that saved many Jews from certain death.
