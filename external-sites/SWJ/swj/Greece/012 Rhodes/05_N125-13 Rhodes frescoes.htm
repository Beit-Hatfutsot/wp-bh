Wall decorations. Elaborate nineteenth-century paintings decorate the walls with floral embellishments, symbols, and inscriptions. Here, a symmetrical garland embraces two elongated Tablets of the Law on which are inscribed the first two of the Ten Commandments: “I am the Lord Thy god," and “You shall have no other Gods beside me.” (Ex.20: 2,3). Three pairs of horns among the vines and flowers: cornucopias represent the classical heritage, a pair of shofarot and two trumpets, perhaps for the Levite musicians in the Temple. A ner tamid hangs above a ribbon banner: “Hear O Israel, the Lord our God, the Lord is one.” (Deut.6: 4). The inscription above the crown on the right reads Keter Torar (an error in restoration), instead of the intended Keter Torah, Torah crown.
