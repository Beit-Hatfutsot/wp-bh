Moise Eliasaf, head of the small Ioannina community, with the oversized old iron key at the synagogue. "This was a mercantile community," he told us in the Alliance School Hebrew of his youth, "where the boys went into business with the fathers. I managed a clothing store, but my son, also Moise Eliasaf, is a doctor in the local hospital. The Alliance School enrolled 500 children before the war, including some Greek and Turkish children… We have few Jewish children today...”
