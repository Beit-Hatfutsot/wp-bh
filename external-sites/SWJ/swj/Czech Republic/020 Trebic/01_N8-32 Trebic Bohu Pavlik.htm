Bohumir Pavlik, an octogenarian who voluntarily cared for the Jewish cemetery in Trebic. A former teacher and school supervisor, he restored the ceremonial hall and maintained the cemetery in his retirement. “I work here every day until darkness comes, and often students come to help me,” he told us. “The Jews of Brno say I am sculpting a monument to myself.” At the entrance to the cemetery, there is a monument to the Jewish soldiers who died in battle in World War I, and another to the 290 Jews of Trebic who perished in World War II.
