<h1>Matrimonial Mischief</h1>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span
style='font-size:28.0pt;font-family:"Monotype Corsiva";mso-bidi-font-family:
Verdana;mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>A<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>500-year
old trail leads from a church in the small Moravian town of Kojetin to today's
Jewish communities in Maidenhead Berkshire, England, and Fort Lauderdale,
Wheeling, Chicago and Philadelphia, in<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span>the U.S.A. The link consists of Torah scrolls from Kojetin's former <i>kehillah</i>
that have found a home abroad. The Maidenhead congregation cherishes its Torah
scroll as an inspiration, while the Protestant congregation in Kojetin
appreciates having its church in the former synagogue building. Maidenhead's
Rabbi<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Jonathan Romain and
Kojetin's Pastor Dobromil Maly have led their communities in the effort to keep
alive the memory of the Jews of Kojetin<i>.<o:p></o:p></i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Expelled from neighbouring Olomouc in 1454, Jews
sought refuge in Kojetin. However, the earliest chronicle referring to Jews in
Kojetin dates from 1566. It lists 56 Jewish families living in the<span
style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span><i>Judengasse. </i>The Jews built their
synagogue of brick and stone at the entrance to the Judengasse, leaving no
record of construction date; there is, however mention of a renovation and
expansion in 1614. Unlike ghetto streets in other towns, this Judengasse was a
clean and cheerful place with abundant meadows and gardens behind the houses.
Most of the Jews were peddlers until they gained permission to engage in
business, but some cultivated grasslands and raised cattle.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Their peace was broken at mid-seventeenth century,
when many Polish and Ukrainian Jews were welcomed into the community after
fleeing from the bloodthirsty hordes of Cossaks under the leadership of Bogdan
Chemielnicki during the years 1648-1649. Absorbed into the community, the new
arrivals introduced Polish prayer rituals and rich Talmudic lore. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>During this period, the synagogue lost its roof in
a fire and waited so for five decades for lack of funds. Although the Jewish
Court Banker, Samuel Wertheimer of Eisenstadt volunteered to pay for restoring
the synagogue, town authorities refused his offer, intending to prevent further
Jewish use of the building on its attractive open square. On this and other
matters, the community petitioned the Chancellery of Prague. In 1718 a
favorable ruling was returned, granting permission to repair the synagogue.
Diminished and impoverished after the fire, the community reorganized. The
ghetto was established and renamed Zidovska Ulice in 1727. Five hundred Jews
inhabited 40 houses. Despite its small size , the <i>kehillah</i> became<span
style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>a center of famous rabbis. One of the
youngest was Rabbi Eleazer Flekeles, a student of the famed Rabbi Ezekiel
Landau-the<i>&quot;Nodah B'Yehuda&quot;.<o:p></o:p></i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoBodyTextIndent>Documents in the local archives illustrate the
social and political changes that Europe was undergoing. The Rescript of the
1727 had prohibited Jews from renting houses that were Christian property. In
1827, the Imperial and Royal District Office addressed a query to the
municipality regarding the observance of the still-in-force Rescript. The
Provisions Office of Kojetin replied, in part:</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;
direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:
Verdana;mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>&quot; <i>In view of the progress of the
nations in the past hundred years&#8230;.it is not surprising that a political law
dating from the age of intolerance</i><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span><i>is no longer in accord with the present age. The Supreme Rescript can
be viewed as no longer meritorious of existence &#8230;.the same should expressly be
rescinded&#8230;..and tolerated Jews should be permitted to rent Christian</i> <i>homes.&quot;<o:p></o:p></i></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>The number of Jews&#8212;443 by 1830&#8212;and their high birth
rate clashed with the legally established limit of 76 families. Ordered in 1830
to investigate reports of possible secret marriages among Jews, the Provisions
Office displayed<i> </i>a pragmatic<i><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span></i>tolerance of infractions. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;
direction:ltr;unicode-bidi:embed'><i><span style='font-family:Verdana;
mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>&quot;Here, single
woman have given birth to children - and since they have no complaints as to
cost of maintenance, they seem to have a secret bond with the father. There are
no definite criteria for proving the existence of a secret and unlawful marriage.
This mischief<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>cannot be countered
by legislative measures. Perhaps the time has come to ease restrictions on
Jewish marriages, since grounds therefore no longer exist.&quot;<o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><i><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:
Verdana;mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>Social and cultural conditions improved in the
second half of the nineteenth century, a veritable golden age for the Jews of
Kojetin. With success in trade, some moved out of the Jewish quarter. But
Zidovska Ulice remained the busy center of social and cultural life, especially
on holidays when it teemed with guests. Dwindling gradually, the <i>kehillah </i>retained
a Jewish<i> </i>mayor until 1924. By 1930 only 32 Jewish families remained. <o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:left;text-indent:35.45pt;direction:ltr;
unicode-bidi:embed'><span style='font-family:Verdana;mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;
mso-fareast-language:EN-US'>During the roundups<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>of 1942 the Nazis captured 83 of the 90 registered Jews and
deported them to Terezienstadt. Few returned. The Nazis had planned to destroy
the synagogue, but local residents demanded permission to use it as a
warehouse. The Protestant Hussite Congregation known as the Czech Brethren
bought the building in 1953 for use as a church. In 1992 the synagogues in the
U.S.A. and England who were recipients of the Torah scrolls, sent
representatives to Kojetin to visit the former synagogue and the cemetery in a
memorial ceremony 50 years after the deportation of Kojetin's Jews.</span><span
style='font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:12.0pt;font-family:Verdana;
mso-bidi-font-family:Verdana;mso-fareast-language:EN-US'><o:p></o:p></span></p>
