Map of Kojetin in 1700, found by Jerusalem residents Avraham and Shlomo Steiner in the Kojetin archives during a search for family roots. Young Steiner’s grandfather’s grandfather was one of the Jews who had to leave Kojetin under Familiant’s Law restrictions, and moved to Bratislava. The map depicts accurately the curving street, Zidovska ulice, with its two rows of Jews’ dwellings running parallel to the moat that encircles the town. Blue marks the Christian houses, and red, the Jewish ones. (Courtesy of A. Steiner. Original in Town Archives, Kojetin.)
