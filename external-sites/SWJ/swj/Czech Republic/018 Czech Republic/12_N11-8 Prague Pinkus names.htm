The names of more than 70,000 Czech Jews murdered in the Shoah were inscribed on the walls of the Pinkus Synagogue in Prague before the renovations of the late 1980s. Here, an official indicates the plaque that temporarily replaced the wall inscriptions. The names were later again inscribed on the walls, but the synagogue was closed to visitors for a long time after being seriously damaged by the floods of August 2002.
