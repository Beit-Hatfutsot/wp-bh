The First Jewish Law of 1938, which aimed to “secure a more effective balance in social and economic life”, was followed by an openly racist Second Law “about the restrictions of the increasing public and economic presence of Jews” (1939). In 1941 the Third Jewish Law prohibited intermarriage and changed the definition of Jew to a racial definition, and in 1942 Jewish religion was no longer an officially accepted denomination. By these laws the deprivation of human rights became complete and Jews were officially separated from the rest of society.
