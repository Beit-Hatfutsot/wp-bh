The International Red Cross, embassies, consulates, and diplomats such as Friedrich Born (1903–1963), Angelo Rotta (1872–1965), Carl (Charles) Lutz (1895–1975), Raoul Wallenberg (1912–1947), Valdemar Langlet (1872–1960), and Giorgio Perlasca (1910–1992) helped to save Jews with fake passports, protective letters and “protected ghettos”.
