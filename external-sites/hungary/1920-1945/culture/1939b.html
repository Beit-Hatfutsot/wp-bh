As a consequence of the First and Second Jewish Laws, Jewish actors lost their work. The theatrical and opera performances held in Goldmark Hall gave them a chance to perform.
“We were deprived of our intellectual work and physical livelihood at the same time. We may have survived the latter, but we choked on the word, which we were forbidden to utter. Work at Goldmark Hall was financially completely insignificant, but this was our pulpit. It is to my satisfaction that we did not use this pulpit for cheap, inferior purposes.” (From Oszkár Beregi’s diary)
