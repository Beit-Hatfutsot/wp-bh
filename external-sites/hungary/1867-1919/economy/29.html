The Textile Factory was founded by Ferenc Goldberger (1750/5–1834) in 1785. In 1825 his son, Sámuel Goldberger (1784/7–1848) took over and modernized the factory with his son, Fülöp. From 1876 Bertold Goldberger (1849–1913) directed the factory. In 1857 the emperor Franz Joseph I (1848–1916) visited it in Óbuda. The family was ennobled in 1867. The picture, possibly taken at the end of the 19th century, shows the members of the Goldberger family: Arnold, Gusztáv, Albert, Gyula, Bertold, Ferenc, Samu and Ödön.
