“Josua Stampfer, son of the Szombathely dayan, made his way on foot from Budapest to Jerusalem through the Balkans, Turkey, Anatolia and Syria, wandering for months in the desert without food or drink until he reached his destination. […] He was an extremely pious man and governed the colony in a religious spirit. Still, he was the one who had once given orders to violate the Sabbath in the synagogue. Once, during the Sabbath prayers, he received news that Bedouins had attacked the colony. Stampfer stopped his recital of the Shmone Esre, threw off his tallit and ordered the men to interrupt their prayers and grab their guns.” (József Patai, A föltámadó Szentföld [The Holy Land Rising Again] Vienna, 1926)
