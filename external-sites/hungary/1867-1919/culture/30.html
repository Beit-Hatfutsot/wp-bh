Born as Ignác Einhorn, he was a rabbi, an enthusiastic supporter of Magyarization, and the leader of the radically modern religious community of Pest in 1848. During the Hungarian Revolution and War of Independence he was an army rabbi. After the fall he left Hungary and married a Catholic woman. In 1869 he returned to Hungary as Ede Horn. Appointed as under-secretary of state in 1875, he was the first government official of Jewish origin and later he became a member of the House of Representatives.
