For many years this inner town cafe was the editorial “office” of A Hét (The Week), the periodical edited by József Kiss. The printing house of the periodical was opposite the cafe.
“Every Friday and Saturday different corners of the cafe were occupied by some gentlemen of shifty appearance who hardly ever turned up on weekdays. They could sit for hours drinking a small glass of beer and busily scribbling lines after lines on sheets of the same size. These were the collaborators of A Hét . Swarming crowd, murmur, the swirl of discussions flying here and there, the thick smoke could not disturb them. […] Here, in the middle of waving, living life […] was A Hét written.” (József Kiss, 1899)
