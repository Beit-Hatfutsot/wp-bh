The Dohány Street Synagogue, designed by the Viennese architect Ludwig Förster (1797–1863), was built between 1854–1859. It combines elements of antique, Byzantine and Moorish styles. With its organ and two steeples resembling those of Catholic churches, the synagogue has become the symbol of the Neolog Jewish citizens of Pest. It is the largest synagogue in Europe that has been continually active.
