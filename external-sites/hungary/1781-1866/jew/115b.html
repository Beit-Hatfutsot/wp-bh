Unknown artist, illustration for the novel Magyar titkok                 (Hungarian Secrets), written by Ignác Nagy

“Rabbits, chickens and other articles to sell are hanging off the country-wagon, peasant-women are sitting on the ground […] who from time to time are casting a glance at the fine, colourful shawls, which an extremely talkative Jew is waving in front of some peasants in wide white linen trousers […]. In the meantime the Jew winks at one of his fellows […] and sometimes he cunningly looks at the rabbits […]. The Jew with the shawls was loudly bargaining with the peasants and his fellow had already tried to slip the third rabbit into his sack when one of the women noticed it […] and started yelling: ’Thief!’” (Ignác Nagy, Magyar titkok)
