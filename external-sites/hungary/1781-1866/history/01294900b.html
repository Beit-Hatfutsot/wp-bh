The participation of the Jews in the Hungarian Revolution and War of Independence caused antisemitic riots in some towns – mainly in Pest and in Pozsony (Pressburg, Bratislava). The mob insisted on the Jews’ exclusion from the National Guard. Later Jews were accepted into the army, and the revolutionary government granted emancipation to the Jews, but this law could not become effective because of the defeat of the War of Independence. During the ensuing reprisal, the Habsburg Empire placed judicial and economic restrictions – payment of reparations – on the Jews.
