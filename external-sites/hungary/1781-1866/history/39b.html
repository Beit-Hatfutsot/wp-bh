Series of maps drawn by Vasquez

The Edict of Tolerance issued by Joseph II allowed the Jews to settle in cities. In Pest the Jewish quarter was in Terézváros, built out of the demolished city wall, close to the marketplace known as Jewish Market. The bustling main street of the Jewish quarter in Pest, Király Street, was the only street linking the inner city with Városliget, the Pest residents’ favorite place for excursions and recreation.
