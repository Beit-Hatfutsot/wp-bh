Joseph II (1780–1790) introduced the Edict of Tolerance (1781). Its section on Jews (Systematica Gentis Judaicae Regulatio, 1783) allowed them to settle in the royal cities (except for the mining towns), but interdicted the use of the Hebrew and Yiddish languages; ordered them to set up Jewish schools under state supervision, allowed Jews to attend universities, learn any trade and engage in commerce, and to lease land. The Edict abolished the Jewish badge.
