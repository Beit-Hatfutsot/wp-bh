The first Jewish physician in Hungary, Oesterreicher was born in Óbuda and took his medical oath in 1782 at the University of Buda. He specialized in mineral waters; in Balatonfüred he founded a hospital that later become famous.

“The Royal University of Buda has received special instructions from His Majesty that the freedom of physicians, in other words, the doctorate of physicians be conferred on non-Catholics in the same manner as on Catholics. Now, on the twenty-first day of the month before Lent this honor has been conferred on József Manes Oesterreicher of the Jewish nation and faith. There can be no doubt that this new order will promote the welfare of the University. The above named physician doctor, who has recently authored an elaborate and fine study on the medicinal waters of Buda and other regions, is from Buda by birth and will herewith become the official physician of the bitter waters of Füred.”
(18th letter of Magyar Hírmondó, dated “second day of Lent” of the year 1782)
