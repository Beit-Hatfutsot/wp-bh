As a respectable and wealthy Jew, Izsák Schulhof married the daughter of the famous rabbi, Ephraim Cohen of Buda, and was a leader of the Jewish community.
He commemorated the events of the 1686 siege of Buda in two mourning songs and in his prosaic work, Megilat Ofen (Chronicle of Buda). In this book he lamented the Turkish rule in Buda and the disaster that befell the Jewish community, which perished during the siege.
