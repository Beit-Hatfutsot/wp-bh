It is likely that Jews accompanying Roman legions appeared in Pannonia, a province of the Roman Empire, in the 1st century C.E., although there is no historic evidence of this. The first evidence of a Jewish community and synagogue in Intercisa (today Dunaújváros) dates back to the 3rd century. The great migration probably swept away the Jews living in Pannonia. In the 10th century they reappeared in the Carpathian Basin; one of the first Jewish communities was in Esztergom around 1050.
