After the laws restricting Jewish settlement and activities, King Béla IV’s Charter of Privileges established the private legal status of Jews, and guaranteed their personal security and their practice of religion. The Jews living in the country became the servants of the Royal Chamber (the Treasury); the office of “Judge of the Jews” (Judex Judaeorum), held by a Christian judge nominated by the king, was instituted. The “Judge of the Jews” dealt with affairs of Jewish property taxes, representation in the government and protection of rights. The Jews repeatedly asked for the confirmation of the charter, which they were granted by succeeding rulers.
