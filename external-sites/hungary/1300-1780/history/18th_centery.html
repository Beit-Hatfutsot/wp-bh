It was the privilege of the big landowners and the smaller nobility to settle the Jews. That is how the Seven Communities and the Jewish community of Óbuda came about; the former were in the western part of Hungary, on the estate of the Esterházy family, the latter on the estate of the Zichy family.
The Seven Communities was the first organizational unity of separate Jewish communities in Hungary. The communities of Eisenstadt (Kismarton), Mattersdorf (Nagymarton), Deutschkreutz (Sopronkeresztúr), Frauenkirchen (Boldogasszony), Kittsee (Köpcsény), Lackenbach (Lakompak), and Kobersdorf (Kabold) belonged to the chief rabbinate of Eisenstadt, and had their own inner administration; their privileges and culture made them prestigious and famous in central and eastern Europe.
