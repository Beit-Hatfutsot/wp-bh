ver since Abraham Goldfaden established the first Yiddish company, theater has played an important role in the artistic and cultural life of the Romanian Jews. They illustrated themselves as actors (Beate Fredanov, N. Stroe, Leib Kanner, Al. Finti, Paul Baratov and many others), directors (Jacob Sternberg), and musicians (H. Malineanu, Elly Roman). Performances were held in Yiddish and Romanian and the repertoire included all genres of classical as well as original productions of plays by local playwrights.
