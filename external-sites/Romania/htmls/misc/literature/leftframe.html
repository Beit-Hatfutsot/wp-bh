They were active in such domains as plastic arts and architecture - Marcel Janco probably being the best known, in the world of the press - Jewish newspapers were among the first to be published in Romania, in music, education, medicine, science etc. More than 250 Jewish writers were active in Romania during the last 100 years. Their writings were published in Romanian, Yiddish, Hungarian, German, French, Hebrew, and other languages and encompassed all literary genres, philosophy, history, and linguistics. Some achieved world fame, like Tristan Tzara (Sami Rosenstein) (1896-1963) and Benjamine Fondane (Fundoianu, Schwartzfeld) (1898-1944), both wrote mostly in French, and the German language poet Paul Celan (Antschel, Ancel) (1920-1970), while the outstanding playwright Mihai Sebastian (Iosef Hechter) (1907-1945), the literary critic Constantin Dobrogeanu-Gherea (1855-1920), the distinguished linguist Alexandru Graur (1900-1988), and many others are acclaimed as important personalities of the Romanian cultural life during the last century.
