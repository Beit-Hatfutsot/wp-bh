he great majority of the Romanian Jews, much as other Eastern European Jews, continued their traditional way of life well into the 20th century. Many local folk traditions interwove with Jewish elements to create a rich array of musical and plastic expressions. For some Romanian Jews, notably among those with an assimilated background, the religious and cultural values of the Judaism were often perceived as "our traditions". Some traditions were created recently, like the Hanukkiadas, as the visits of the few rabbis and other leaders remaining in the country to small communities during Hanukkah festival came to be known.
