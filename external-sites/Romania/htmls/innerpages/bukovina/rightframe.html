This former Moldavian province was part of the Austria-Hungary Empire until WW1. Jews were granted Austrian citizenship in 1867. They settled in small towns, while Czernowitz hosted the largest Jewish community of the region. A center of Hasidism (Sadagura, Vizhnitz), Bukovina was also home for a large group of Jews who adopted German language and culture, a trend that was strongest especially among the wealthy secular upper middle class of Czernowitz who liked to consider itself as the "Vienna of Eastern Europe".
