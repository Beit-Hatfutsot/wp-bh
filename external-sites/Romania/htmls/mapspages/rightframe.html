The Jewish population of Romania is estimated at less than 10,000 - the majority aged over 70 and no more than 1,000 under 25 years of age. Today more than half of the Romanian Jews live in Bucharest, but some small communities still exist in Jassy, Cluj, Timisoara, Arad, Oradea, Satu Mare, Dorohoi, Suceava, Constanta, Targu Mures, Radauti, and in other towns.
